Tips for Creating Effective Feats

Start Small

Why: Building the habit of creating and completing feats is more important than creating the "perfect" feat.

How:
First week: Create 2-3 simple, recurring feats
Build from there as you get comfortable
Add complexity gradually

---

Use Consistent Naming

Why: Makes your feat list scannable and reduces cognitive load.

How:
Use similar structures: "Complete [subject] homework"
Group related feats: "Morning: [action]"
Consistent verbs: Always "Practice" for music, "Review" for studying

---

Make "Done" Clear

Why: Ambiguity prevents completion and causes stress.

How:
‚úÖ "Complete math worksheet (pages 12-15)"
‚úÖ "Practice piano: scales + one song"
‚ùå "Do some homework" (vague!)
‚ùå "Work on project" (no definition of done!)

---

Balance Difficulty

Why: Too many Epic feats = overwhelm. Too many Easy feats = slow progress.

Sweet spot:
60-70% Easy/Medium (daily maintenance)
20-30% Hard (meaningful progress)
5-10% Epic (major accomplishments)

Example daily list:
3-4 Easy feats (morning routine, quick tasks)
2-3 Medium feats (homework, chores)
1 Hard feat (study session, project work)
0-1 Epic feat (only if you have time!)

---

Create Feats in Batches

Why: Reduces decision fatigue and planning time.

How:
Sunday evening: Create all week's feats
Monthly: Set up recurring templates
Seasonally: Adjust for schedule changes

Don't overthink: You can always add/delete feats throughout the week.

---

Common Feat Creation Mistakes

Mistake #1: Feats Too Big

Problem: "Complete all homework" is overwhelming.

Solution: Break it down:
"Complete math homework"
"Read history chapter"
"Practice Spanish vocab"

Or use: AI Feat Breakdown!

---

Mistake #2: Feats Too Vague

Problem: "Be productive" doesn't tell you what to do.

Solution: Make it specific:
"Write 500 words for essay"
"Answer client emails"
"Organize desk drawer"

---

Mistake #3: Too Many Feats

Problem: Creating 20 feats for tomorrow sets you up for failure.

Solution: Start with 3-5 essential feats. Add more only if you finish these.

Remember: Completing 3/3 feels better than completing 5/20.

---

Mistake #4: Perfectionism

Problem: Spending 10 minutes crafting the perfect feat title.

Solution: Create it in 30 seconds. Adjust later if needed. Action > perfection.

---

Mistake #5: Forgetting Categories

Problem: All feats are uncategorized chaos.

Solution: Always assign Morning, School, After-school, or Nighttime. Helps with time awareness!
